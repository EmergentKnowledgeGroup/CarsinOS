## Status vocabulary

- `[ ]` pending or not proven.
- `[x]` implemented and proven by the stated gate.
- `[x] HISTORICAL-PASS` means version 1.0 implementation/proof is retained because the amended requirement did not redesign that row. It does not make a reopened dependency or version 1.1 section green; downstream version 1.1 work remains blocked until every reopened dependency and the section revalidation row pass. If revalidation fails, the row is reopened.
- `BLOCKED` means a matching open blockerboard entry prevents useful progress.
- Every major section ends with a full regression and checkpoint update.

## Change-control rule

Version 1.0 completion proof remains historical only. Version 1.1 explicitly reopens EA-001, EA-002, EA-005, EA-101, EA-102, EA-103, EA-106, and EA-113. EA-003 and EA-004 remain current independent proof. EA-104, EA-105, and EA-107 through EA-112 are retained as `HISTORICAL-PASS`; they neither satisfy reopened dependencies nor make EA-10 green until EA-101/102/103/106 and the EA-113 revalidation pass. Any retained row whose regression fails is immediately reopened. After version 1.1 lock, a material requirement change updates the specification, affected checklist rows, and blockerboard in one reviewed batch; records rationale and owner decision; reruns affected gates; and appends a checkpoint. No checkbox may be removed or weakened to obtain green.
